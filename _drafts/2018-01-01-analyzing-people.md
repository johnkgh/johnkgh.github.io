I don't think anyone really knows anything about themselves

<!-- more -->

Why people say things is often more telling than what they're saying

There's a trend I've noticed with people who've announced themselves to be the smartest person in the room.

They're more self-aware than the rest of us.

If they're they don't have the highest raw intellectual power in the room, at least they have the feeling of being wiser.

I'm always wary of opening my mouth with the intention of communicating that **_I_** have something important and original to say, as if I'm experiencing consciousness on a separate plane than **_you_** are, with some unique point of view that **_you_** have never considered, sharing merely a simple yet astute observation for **_me_** that easily glanced over **_your_** benighted mind. If I'm being honest, I find these types of people rather insufferable in their arrogance, though I'm always open to ~~being talked down to~~ hearing what they have to say.

I wrote and rewrote this post a dozen times describing my feelings of derision toward personal blogs because I believe, perhaps falsely, that they are mostly written by these types of people, though I know I'm not being completely fair in my assessment. Nevertheless, I sincerely don't want to be the embodiment of that caricature for my millions of avid readers.

Going forward, I hope that this blog isn't meant to be preachy or express some profound ideas; it's just a guy practicing his writing and giving his thoughts from time to time on various subjects. It's really for my own edification and selfish ingratiation. If you're here however, I hope you like what you read.

[1] The irony of this quote is that in its usage, the messenger most likely has in himself or herself the intention of conveying that he or she is unique from everyone else by the mere act of recognizing and acknowledging the idea that individuals are not so individual in their beliefs about themselves relative to others. Wallace had an uncanny ability of capturing the meta-narrative through various paradoxes, and I can only imagine that he was well aware of the implications of this quote in his own life.
